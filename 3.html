Location & Campus Environment:

Ithaca College is situated in the heart of the Finger Lakes region, offering students a unique blend of academic excellence and natural beauty. The campus is surrounded by the scenic gorges, wineries, and lakes of Central New York, providing a picturesque environment for learning and exploration. The college's location near downtown Ithaca and Cornell University adds to the vibrant college town atmosphere, with easy access to cultural events and recreational activities. The campus features a variety of facilities, including dining halls, student centers, and athletic facilities, catering to the diverse needs of the student body. With a commitment to inclusivity and community engagement, Ithaca College fosters an environment where students can thrive both academically and personally..

Housing & Dining:

The College dining hall provides a wide choice of hot and cold entrées, vegetables, fruits, salads, and desserts. Foods are served cafeteria-style with unlimited helpings. Prepaid weekly meal plans (including breakfast, lunch, and dinner) are available. Two-week plans include lunch and dinner on Saturday between sessions.
It is also possible to purchase individual meal tickets at the door.

Class Size & Student-Faculty Ratio:

Ithaca College has a student-faculty ratio of 11:1. Additionally, another source mentions a student-to-faculty ratio of 10:1. This indicates that there are approximately 11 students for every faculty member at the college.

Accreditation & Reputation:

Ithaca College is an accredited institution and a member of the Middle States Commission on Higher Education (MSCHE). Its accreditation status is reaffirmed, as confirmed by the most recent action on June 21, 2018. Additionally, the College's School of Business has been reaccredited by The Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB), recognizing its commitment to quality education and student success. This accreditation is important for students considering transfer credits or post-graduate programs.

Graduation & Retention Rates:

Ithaca College Graduation & Retention Rates Ithaca College has a strong commitment to student success, with impressive graduation and retention rates. Here are some key statistics: Graduation Rate: 74% within 150% normal time (6 years for a 4-year degree)
Retention Rate: 83% for full-time students
6-Year Graduation Rate: 77%
4-Year Graduation Rate: 72%
Non-Completers Rate: 23% of the cohort did not complete their degree within eight years
These rates indicate that Ithaca College is effective in helping students achieve their academic goals and returning to campus for their sophomore year. The college's career services and job placement support further enhance the employability of its graduates.
